## Password Policy Compliance

### What is a Password Policy?

A password policy defines the rules and requirements for creating and managing passwords within an organization. A strong password policy helps ensure that users create complex and secure passwords, reducing the risk of unauthorized access.

### Why Follow Industry Standards?

Following industry standards, such as those outlined by NIST SP 800-63B, ensures that your password policy is robust and aligned with best practices. Weak password policies can make it easier for attackers to guess or crack passwords.

### How to Implement a Strong Password Policy

Here’s an example of a strong password policy based on NIST guidelines:

1. **Minimum Length**: At least 8 characters.
2. **Character Variety**: Require a mix of uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special characters.
3. **No Common Patterns**: Disallow common patterns like "123456" or "qwerty".
4. **Password History**: Prevent reuse of previous passwords.
5. **Complexity Requirements**: Avoid overly complex rules that discourage users from creating strong passwords.

### Real-World Example: Yahoo Breach (CVE-2014-0001)

In 2014, Yahoo suffered a massive data breach affecting all 3 billion user accounts. One of the contributing factors was a weak password policy, allowing users to create simple and easily guessable passwords. Implementing a strong password policy could have reduced the impact of the breach.

### How to Prevent / Defend

#### Secure Password Policy Practices

1. **Follow NIST Guidelines**: Ensure your password policy aligns with NIST SP 800-63B.
2. **Educate Users**: Provide guidance on creating strong passwords and the importance of following the policy.
