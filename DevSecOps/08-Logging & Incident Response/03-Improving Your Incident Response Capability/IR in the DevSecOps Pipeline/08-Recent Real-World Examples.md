## Recent Real-World Examples

### Example 1: SolarWinds Supply Chain Attack (CVE-2020-1014)

In December 2020, SolarWinds disclosed a supply chain attack where attackers compromised the SolarWinds software update mechanism. This allowed them to distribute malware to customers, including government agencies and large corporations.

**Impact**: The attack affected numerous organizations, leading to significant data breaches and operational disruptions.

**Lessons Learned**: This incident highlighted the importance of continuous monitoring and incident response capabilities. Organizations should have robust mechanisms in place to detect and respond to such attacks quickly.

### Example 2: Colonial Pipeline Ransomware Attack (May 2021)

In May 2021, Colonial Pipeline suffered a ransomware attack that disrupted fuel supplies across the eastern United States. The attackers exploited a compromised password to gain access to the company's network.

**Impact**: The attack caused widespread panic and led to temporary fuel shortages.

**Lessons Learned**: This incident underscored the need for strong password policies and continuous monitoring of network activities. Effective incident response plans could have minimized the impact of the attack.
