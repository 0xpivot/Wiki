## Introduction to DevSecOps

### What is DevSecOps?

DevSecOps is an approach to software development that integrates security practices into the entire DevOps lifecycle. Traditionally, security was often treated as an afterthought, added late in the development process. In contrast, DevSecOps aims to embed security at every stage, from planning and coding to testing and deployment. This ensures that security is not just a concern for the security team but a shared responsibility across all teams involved in the software development process.

### Why DevSecOps Matters

The importance of DevSecOps cannot be overstated, especially given the increasing number of cyber threats and data breaches. According to the 2021 Verizon Data Breach Investigations Report, 85% of breaches involved weak, default, or stolen passwords. Integrating security into the DevOps pipeline helps organizations identify and mitigate vulnerabilities early, reducing the risk of such breaches.

### How DevSecOps Works

DevSecOps operates on the principle of continuous integration and continuous delivery (CI/CD). By embedding security checks into the CI/CD pipeline, teams can catch and address security issues as they arise, rather than waiting until the end of the development cycle. This proactive approach not only improves security but also enhances the overall quality and reliability of the software.
