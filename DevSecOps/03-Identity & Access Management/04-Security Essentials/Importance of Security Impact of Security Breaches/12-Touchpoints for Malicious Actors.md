## Touchpoints for Malicious Actors

Malicious actors can exploit various touchpoints to gain unauthorized access to a company's assets. These touchpoints include:

- **Physical Premises**: Entry points such as doors, windows, and parking lots.
- **Online Systems**: Network interfaces, server APIs, and application endpoints.
- **Employee Interactions**: Social engineering tactics such as phishing and pretexting.
- **Third-Party Services**: External vendors and partners who have access to company systems.

### Physical Premises

Physical premises can be vulnerable to attacks such as:

- **Tailgating**: Unauthorized individuals following authorized personnel into restricted areas.
- **Lockpicking**: Gaining access to locked areas through lockpicking techniques.
- **Social Engineering**: Manipulating employees to gain access to restricted areas.

#### Example: Tailgating Attack

In 2018, a security researcher demonstrated how easy it was to tailgate into a major tech company's headquarters. This incident highlighted the need for robust physical security measures.

### Online Systems

Online systems can be vulnerable to attacks such as:

- **Phishing Attacks**: Sending fraudulent emails to trick employees into revealing sensitive information.
- **SQL Injection**: Exploiting vulnerabilities in web applications to execute arbitrary SQL commands.
- **Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)**: Injecting malicious scripts into web pages viewed by other users.
- **Remote Code Execution (RCE)**: Exploiting vulnerabilities to execute arbitrary code on remote systems.

#### Example: SQL Injection Attack

In 2020, a SQL injection vulnerability in a popular CMS led to the compromise of numerous websites. This incident underscores the importance of securing online systems against such attacks.

### Employee Interactions

Employee interactions can be vulnerable to attacks such as:

- **Phishing**: Sending fraudulent emails to trick employees into revealing sensitive information.
- **Pretexting**: Creating a fabricated scenario to manipulate employees into providing sensitive information.
- **Baiting**: Leaving infected USB drives or other media in public areas to entice employees to plug them into their computers.

#### Example: Phishing Attack

In 2019, a large financial institution fell victim to a sophisticated phishing campaign that resulted in significant financial losses. This incident highlights the need for robust employee training and awareness programs.

### Third-Party Services

Third-party services can be vulnerable to attacks such as:

- **Supply Chain Attacks**: Compromising third-party vendors to gain access to company systems.
- **API Vulnerabilities**: Exploiting vulnerabilities in APIs to gain unauthorized access to company systems.
- **Data Leakage**: Inadequate security measures leading to the exposure of sensitive data.

#### Example: Supply Chain Attack

The 2021 SolarWinds supply chain attack compromised numerous organizations by exploiting a vulnerability in the SolarWinds Orion software. This incident underscores the critical nature of securing third-party services.
