## Introduction to CIS Benchmarks

The Center for Internet Security (CIS) is a community-driven nonprofit organization founded in the year 2000. Its primary mission is to promote the highest level of cybersecurity among organizations of all sizes and across various industries. One of the key contributions of CIS is the development of CIS Controls and Benchmarks, which are widely recognized as best practices for securing IT systems and data.

### What Are CIS Controls and Benchmarks?

CIS Controls are a set of prioritized and actionable high-impact security measures designed to protect against the most prevalent attacks. These controls are organized into three categories: Basic, Foundational, and Organizational. Each category builds upon the previous one, providing a comprehensive framework for improving an organization's security posture.

CIS Benchmarks, on the other hand, are detailed configuration guidelines for specific technologies. They provide a standardized way to secure operating systems, applications, and network devices. These benchmarks are globally recognized and are used by governments and organizations worldwide to ensure that their IT systems and services are as secure as possible.

### Importance of CIS Benchmarks

Governments and organizations, such as the United States Department of Defense, rely on CIS Benchmarks to ensure compliance with security standards. By following these benchmarks, organizations can evaluate whether their systems are properly hardened and secured, rather than relying on guesswork.

Each benchmark includes an audit and remediation process that guides users through the steps required to achieve compliance. This process ensures that organizations can systematically assess their current security posture and take corrective actions to meet the recommended configurations.

### Official CIS Website

To access the CIS Benchmarks, visit the official CIS website at [https://www.cisecurity.org](https://www.cisecurity.org). Here, you will find benchmarks for more than 100 different technologies, covering a wide range of operating systems, applications, and network devices.
