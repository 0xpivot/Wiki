## Introduction to DevSecOps

### What is DevSecOps?

DevSecOps is a methodology that integrates security practices into the DevOps pipeline. Traditionally, security was often treated as an afterthought, leading to vulnerabilities that could take weeks, months, or even years to address. This delay in fixing vulnerabilities exposes organizations and their customers to significant risks. DevSecOps aims to shift security left, meaning it is integrated early in the development process, rather than being a separate phase at the end.

### Why is DevSecOps Important?

Security vulnerabilities are essentially bugs and feature requests. Just like any other bug, they can take a considerable amount of time to be identified, prioritized, and fixed. This delay in addressing vulnerabilities creates a prolonged period of risk exposure. In contrast, DevSecOps ensures that security is a shared responsibility among all team members, not just the security team. This collaborative approach helps in identifying and fixing vulnerabilities more quickly, reducing the overall risk exposure.

### How Does DevSecOps Work?

When DevSecOps is implemented effectively, the engineers who write the code are responsible for fixing the issues they introduce. Similarly, the operations team ensures that all systems are fully patched before deployment. This shared responsibility model fosters a culture of security awareness and collaboration throughout the organization.
