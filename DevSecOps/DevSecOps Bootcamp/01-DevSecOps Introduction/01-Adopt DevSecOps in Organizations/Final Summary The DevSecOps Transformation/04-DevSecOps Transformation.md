## DevSecOps Transformation

DevSecOps aims to address these issues by fundamentally changing how teams work together and interact. The core principle of DevSecOps is to integrate security practices throughout the entire software development lifecycle, rather than treating it as an afterthought.

### Breaking Down Barriers

DevSecOps breaks down the barriers between development, operations, and security teams. Instead of working in isolation, these teams collaborate closely, sharing knowledge and responsibilities. This shift fosters a culture where everyone is accountable for building secure software and systems.

### Early Security Consideration

By integrating security from the very beginning, DevSecOps ensures that potential vulnerabilities are identified and addressed early in the development process. This proactive approach helps prevent security issues from reaching production, reducing the likelihood of costly and time-consuming fixes later on.

### Proactive Operations

Operations teams become more proactive in maintaining a secure environment. They work closely with developers and security experts to ensure that the infrastructure is robust and resilient against attacks. This collaboration helps identify and mitigate risks before they can cause harm.

### Real-World Example: Capital One Breach (CVE-2019-11510)

Another significant breach that underscores the importance of DevSecOps is the Capital One breach in 2019. The attacker exploited a misconfigured web application firewall (WAF) to access sensitive customer data. This breach could have been prevented if security was integrated into the development process from the start. A DevSecOps approach would have ensured that the WAF was properly configured and monitored, reducing the risk of such vulnerabilities.
