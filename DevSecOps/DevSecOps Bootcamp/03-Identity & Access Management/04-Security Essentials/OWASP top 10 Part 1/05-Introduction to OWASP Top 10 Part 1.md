## Introduction to OWASP Top 10

The Open Web Application Security Project (OWASP) is a non-profit organization dedicated to improving the security of software. One of its most significant contributions is the OWASP Top 10, a list of the most critical web application security risks. This list is updated periodically to reflect the evolving landscape of web application security threats. As of 2021, the latest version of the OWASP Top 10 provides a comprehensive overview of the most prevalent security issues faced by web applications today.

### Importance of OWASP Top 10

The OWASP Top 10 serves as a guideline for developers, security professionals, and organizations to prioritize their efforts in securing web applications. By focusing on the most common and impactful vulnerabilities, teams can allocate resources more effectively and reduce the risk of security breaches.

#### Historical Context

The OWASP Top 10 has been updated several times since its inception. The 2021 version is the most recent update, following the 2017 edition. Each update reflects changes in the threat landscape, new technologies, and emerging security practices. The transition from 2017 to 2021 involved a reorganization and refinement of the categories to better align with current security challenges.

### Structure of OWASP Top 10

The OWASP Top 10 is structured in a specific order of importance. This ordering is not arbitrary; it is based on data collected from real-world security incidents, the frequency of attacks, and the prevalence of vulnerabilities across various applications. Understanding this structure helps prioritize security efforts and focus on the most critical areas first.

#### Data-Driven Approach

Eight out of the ten categories in the OWASP Top 10 are derived from empirical data. This data-driven approach ensures that the list remains relevant and reflective of the actual security threats faced by web applications. The remaining two categories are based on expert consensus and emerging trends.

### Overview of the Categories

The OWASP Top 10 consists of ten categories, each representing a distinct type of security vulnerability. These categories are:

1. **Injection**
2. **Broken Authentication**
3. **Sensitive Data Exposure**
4. **XML External Entities (XXE)**
5. **Broken Access Control**
6. **Security Misconfiguration**
7. **Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)**
8. **Insecure Deserialization**
9. **Using Components with Known Vulnerabilities**
10. **Insufficient Logging & Monitoring**

Each category will be explored in detail, including its definition, potential impacts, real-world examples, and preventive measures.
