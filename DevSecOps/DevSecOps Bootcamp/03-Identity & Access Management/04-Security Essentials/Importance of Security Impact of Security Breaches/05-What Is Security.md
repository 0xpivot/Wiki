## What Is Security?

Security is fundamentally about protecting assets from malicious actors. These assets can range from physical property such as office supplies and documents to digital assets like customer data, intellectual property, and operational systems. The goal of security is to ensure that these assets remain safe and accessible only to authorized individuals.

### Physical Security

Physical security involves securing tangible assets within a company's premises. This includes:

- **Building Access**: Ensuring that unauthorized individuals cannot enter the building. This can be achieved through measures such as keycard access, biometric scanners, and security personnel.
- **Office Supplies and Documents**: Protecting physical documents and office supplies from theft or damage. This can involve locking cabinets, using shredders for sensitive documents, and implementing strict access controls.

#### Example: Physical Security Breach

In 2017, a former employee of Equifax stole sensitive information by physically accessing the company's servers. This breach highlights the importance of robust physical security measures.

### Digital Security

Digital security encompasses the protection of online assets and systems. This includes:

- **Network Security**: Protecting the company's internal network from unauthorized access and attacks.
- **Server Security**: Securing servers to prevent data breaches and system compromises.
- **Database Security**: Ensuring that databases containing sensitive information are protected from unauthorized access and tampering.
- **Application Security**: Protecting applications from vulnerabilities that could be exploited by attackers.
- **Employee Devices**: Securing devices used by employees, including laptops, smartphones, and tablets.
- **Printers and Other Connected Devices**: Ensuring that all devices connected to the network are secure and cannot be used as entry points for attacks.

#### Example: Digital Security Breach

In 2021, the SolarWinds supply chain attack compromised numerous organizations, including government agencies and private companies. This breach underscores the critical nature of digital security and the potential impact of a single vulnerability.
